## Featured: `/subtask` (Lightweight In-Session Fork)

New in v2.1.212 (July 17): when `/fork` was upgraded to create full **background sessions** (with their own row in `claude agents`), the old in-session forking behavior was preserved as **`/subtask`**.

**What it does:** `/subtask` creates a conversational fork that runs as a subagent *within* your current session. It inherits your full conversation context but works independently — useful for exploring an alternative approach, running a side investigation, or testing a hypothesis without polluting your main thread.

**When to use which:**
- **`/fork`** — when you want a fully independent session that persists in `claude agents`, can run in the background, and survives after you close the parent session.
- **`/subtask`** — when you want a quick, disposable exploration that stays inside your current session. The subtask's findings are summarized back into your main conversation when it finishes.

**Example:**
```
> /subtask investigate whether switching from axios to fetch would break our retry logic
```
Claude spins up an in-session subagent that reads the retry code, analyzes compatibility, and reports back — while your main conversation stays clean and focused.
