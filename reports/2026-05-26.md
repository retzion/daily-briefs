## Featured: Rewind Menu — Selective Summarization

The Rewind menu's **"Summarize from here"** and **"Summarize up to here"** options let you surgically compress portions of your conversation without losing everything.

**Why it's useful:** After a long debugging session, you often have pages of failed attempts followed by the working solution. Rather than `/compact` (which summarizes the entire conversation) or `/clear` (which wipes everything), selective summarization lets you compress just the noisy parts while keeping critical context intact. Users report reclaiming 80–90% of tokens from verbose debugging sessions while preserving project narrative.

**How to use it:**
1. Press `Esc` twice (when input is empty) or type `/rewind` to open the Rewind menu
2. Select the message checkpoint you want to summarize around
3. Choose **"Summarize from here"** (compress everything after this point) or **"Summarize up to here"** (compress everything before this point)

**Example workflow:** You've spent 40 messages debugging a flaky test. The fix is in the last 5 messages. Open the Rewind menu, select the message just before the fix, choose "Summarize up to here" — all the failed attempts collapse into a brief summary, and you keep full context on the working solution.
